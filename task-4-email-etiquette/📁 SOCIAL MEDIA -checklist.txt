?? SOCIAL MEDIA DO’s AND DON’Ts
? DO’s
* Use strong and unique passwords 
* Keep your profile professional 
* Verify information before sharing 
* Respect others online 
* Use privacy settings properly 

? DON’Ts
* Do not share personal or sensitive information 
* Do not click on suspicious links 
* Do not post offensive or inappropriate content 
* Do not engage in cyberbullying 
* Do not accept unknown friend requests 

?? SHORT WRITE-UP (100–150 words)
Poor digital communication can lead to misunderstandings and missed opportunities. For example, if a student sends an email to a professor without a subject line, proper greeting, or clear message, the email may be ignored or misunderstood. Using informal language such as “Hey” or “Send fast” can also create a negative impression.
In such situations, the problem arises due to lack of professionalism and clarity. This can be avoided by following proper email etiquette, such as writing a clear subject line, using respectful language, and structuring the message properly.
By improving communication skills and being mindful of tone and format, students can ensure that their messages are taken seriously and responded to positively.
