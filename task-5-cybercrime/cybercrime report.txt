A phishing attack is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that look legitimate.

For example, a student receives an email that appears to be from their bank, asking them to update their account details urgently. The email contains a link that redirects to a fake website that looks exactly like the bank’s official page. When the student enters their login credentials, the attacker captures the information and gains access to the bank account.

Phishing attacks target individuals who are unaware or less cautious while using the internet. The consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

These attacks are dangerous because they appear very real and create a sense of urgency. Therefore, users must always verify the source before clicking on links or sharing any personal information online.


CYBER SAFETY CHECKLIST:

- Use strong and unique passwords for all accounts
- Do not share OTPs, passwords, or personal information with anyone
- Avoid clicking on unknown or suspicious links
- Check website URLs carefully before entering details
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
- Keep your devices and apps updated regularly
- Do not download files from untrusted sources
- Be cautious of messages asking for urgent action

UPI SAFETY TIPS:
- Never share your UPI PIN with anyone
- Do not approve unknown payment requests
